“We are all in the gutter, but some of us are looking at the games”

To begin I want you to close your eyes. (That’s not how reading works, come on Kevin). In spring of 2019 I was in a dark place. I was a 29-year-old nerd waiting tables with a bunch of college kids, some of whom had the audacity to openly and optimistically embrace the experience that is life in the presence of a 29-year-old depressed phony (me) who spent as much time brooding as I did secretly hoping they’d ask me to tag along sometime. Sometimes they did. Sometimes I said yes. They were (and probably still are) nice people, and if I tried harder, I may have made friends. All I had to do is accept that sometimes people like me, even when I really don’t.

I had received an Xbox 360 for Christmas in 2013 and had lost it in late 2017, which is a story that belongs in a different paragraph in a different document. In the fall of 2018, I bought a PS4. I had stopped playing games in the years between 2015 and that day in 2018. I felt like either I stopped liking them, or they stopped making games that I liked. I bought this PS4 because of several memories I regularly recall when I think of what my therapist at the time referred to as, “happy thoughts”. Playing the PlayStation Underground demo of Metal Gear Solid late on a Friday night in 2000 with my childhood friends, making Snake smoke cigarettes and wasting ammo because we were ten and it was cool (you heard me right Jack Thompson who is as qualified to practice law as I am right now). Memories of playing Gran Turismo with my younger brother (who is now a game developer) and making fun of him for moving the controller in whatever direction he was turning the vehicle (he’s a PC guy who still cannot use a controller). I wanted those times, even if I had to find them alone. Without friends. Without anyone. I downloaded PS Now and began catching up on some past releases I, for one reason or another, never got to play. Uncharted 1, 2, and 3. The Last of Us (which some believe is a pretty good game). Arkham Asylum, Assassins Creed Black Flag, Red Dead Redemption, Assassins Creed Unity (which is the best Assassins Creed. I won’t die on that hill, but I will lease property on it), and of course the Metal Gear Solid HD collection which did not include the first MGS but you can smoke in MGS3 as well as do…other stuff. Just as I remembered, video games are fun. Playing them was fun.
