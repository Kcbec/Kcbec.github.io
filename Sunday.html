TIME, PRECIOUS TIME


By Just a Bald Guy

Follow @justabaldguy

If I ranked the best times of the week, it’d play a lot like I think you’d expect. I’d have a nice slide, if it was a graph. Friday at 4:15 or so, when I’m walking out of the parking lot, as the pinnacle. It hangs out there and eventually slides down.

The graph is in “military time” because when I was little and vulnerable to the jingoistic media I consumed I thought it was cool. Fortunately as I aged I realized many normal people use it too in other parts of the country. Finally, I think it looks cleaner than a bunch of “pm” and “am” times in the graph.



NOT LOOKING BACK, BUT I WANT TO LOOK AROUND ME NOW


So what’s going on? I suppose it starts with what I expect versus what I get. On Friday, doing the Fred Flinstone slide to my car (older reference lost on younger readers) the world is my burrito and I’m ready to seize it. I listen to Rush on my way home from work (every Friday is “Rush Friday”) and think about what I’d like to accomplish. There’s a slight dip lately due to rough marriage stuff but otherwise it’s nice.

Saturday I wake up around 7:30, get a cup of coffee, and check in online. During the week I rarely get the time, so this is Discord updates to see what my online friends (pretty much my only friends) are doing. I might catch an episode of JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure or a LOFOES on bike lanes in other countries or whatever current obsession is consuming me. Then I fire up Fallout ‘76 and stream to three or four folks on Twitch.

I grab a nap around noon, as my teen daughters are getting up. Waking up to lunch along with more anime or YouTube, and it’s great. This is my alone time, it’s my me time, it’s time I get to be almost 100% in control (family obligations pending). By the end of Saturday, I’m looking at yard work most days of the year, but that’s not bad as it’s good time to rock out and be in the sunshine (stupid body and its inability to convert cholesterol into useful vitamins without ultraviolet light from the giant ball of heat that makes me sweat just walking out to the mailbox). The wife usually cooks a good Saturday meal, then I might pound the skins a bit (that’s literally playing the drums, not an innuendo (obligatory “giggity.gif” reference just in case (hurray for nested parentheses))).


TIME, WHY YOU PUNISH ME?


So then to Sunday. Church in the morning isn’t typically exciting, but it’s fine. I start laundry when I get home and grab a nap around 11:30. Still decent. Usually I’m eating lunch and streaming something again by 1pm or so, then maybe two hours of gaming. Then the bottom drops out.

Around 3pm, I’m starting to get the sense in the back of my skull. It’s about time to finish the laundry. I usually do 30-45 minutes on the treadmill, enjoying random YouTube essays. It’s ok, but dropping. Vacuum the floor, put clothes away, get ready for dinner (always leftovers). It’s about the end now. The night is kinda spent in flux, a little easier with football but either way not good. Monday’s on the horizon (literally).


TIME MARCHES ON


I’ve been this way for years. Decades, even. It started around high school, but was a bit better during college. After all, college is fun and wasted time, so though I wasn’t excited about school it was a few hours a day of something I could fake my way through on the worst of days, and fail to impress girls on the best. A few hours of college then I was home playing Wrestlemania 2000 on my N64 or watching the Empire Strike Back while waiting for my roommate to hang up so I could dial in to the interwebz.

If you wonder, yes I’ve been officially diagnosed with generalized anxiety. Yes I’m in counseling for it, and yes I’m on medication for it. And yes, I’ve seen some relief. The summers are bliss. Nights before Mondays off are fine. And my job, particularly this year, isn’t even that bad. Just a mental block about losing the freedom to pee when I want or check the message on discord (it was just an @here so false alarm).

I’m working through a litany of mindfulness exercises, which is working well. I’m trying to find things to look forward to every day, especially Sunday evening or Monday afternoon. I’ve enjoyed saving podcasts for the drive to work as an incentive to get lost in the day and live in the moment. And I constantly look for new careers to either work from home or offer more daily freedom than the minute-to-minute whizbang world of teaching 7th graders about the endocrine

Any others out there relate? Would it be immediately better without a job? Would even my ideal job trigger this bout of annoyance every Sunday? Let’s with a cliched saying: only time will tell.
